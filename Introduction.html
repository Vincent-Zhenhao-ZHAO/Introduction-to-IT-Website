The History of AI

Various mathematicians, scientists and philosophers toyed with the idea of artifiical intelligence during the 1950's. One of these people was Alan Turing, a British mathematician, who believed that if humans were able to receive information and make logical decisons to solve problems, then why couldn't machines. This led him to write the paper 'Computing Machinery and Intelligence' in 1950, detailing how he believed intelligent machines could be built, and how we could determine their intelligence to see if a a machine could pass as a human; this is done using the Turing test.

As computers began to be more accessible due to their lower cost, they also became faster and capable of storing much more information. Since the release of Turing's research paper and the famous Dartmouth conference in 1955 (in which John McCArthy coined the term artificial intelligence), AI has has developed tremendously, to the point where some machine are now able to pass the Turing test, and can hold quality conversations with humans. How AI is used in the modern world

AI is gradually entering every aspect of modern life; whether it be face ID on the latest iPhones, smart thermostats that automatically change the temperature in your home or even the recommendation feature on Netflix. Many of the world's greatest companies are looking at how AI can further improve our lives, by making current technology and machines safer and more efficient. For example, Tesla (amongst most car companies) are attempting to make cars fully autonomous, in order to drastically reduce the number of car accidents - this is done via machine learning and complex algorithms. In order to make the production process more efficient for many companies, some low key skilled workers are being replaced by machines that are much quicker and more accurate when performing their jobs; this can make the cost per unit output much cheaper for companies, saving them a lot of money. AI is also used in the finance world, predicting crashes in the stock market by noticing patterns and analysing large amounts of data incredibly quickly, allowing investment banks to alter their plans for the future in order to avoid losing large sums of money. The uses for AI is incredibly vast, and they will continue to increase for years to come.
